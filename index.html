About Me

I’m Shreenandan Sahu, a passionate developer of indigenous medical technologies aimed at addressing India's unique healthcare needs. I focus on designing and developing active biomedical devices—such as rehabilitation and diagnostic tools—as well as computational systems including mobile-based VR platforms for assessment and therapy. My work blends embedded hardware with software to create accessible,real-world solutions tailored for Indian contexts.

EKOBIT: electronics stethoscope

Designed and developed a low‐cost electronic stethoscope with dual‐microphone adaptive noise cancellation, analog front‐end (AFE) filtering, and Python‐based real‐time signal visualization for heart sound.

COHMET: ePEG board for stroke rehab

Designed and developed an ePEG board, a portable rehabilitation device integrating ATmega328P‐based custom PCB, Bluetooth communication, and real‐time response tracking via an Android app.

VRNAV: hand held VR navigation device

Built a Unity‐based mobile VR experience with an ATmega328P+HC‐05 based universal handheld controller for navigating in the 3D reconstructions of Konark sun temple of Odisha
